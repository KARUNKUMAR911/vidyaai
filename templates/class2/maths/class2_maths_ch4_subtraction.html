Subtraction With Borrowing
When the top Units digit is SMALLER — borrow a Ten!

When Do We Borrow?

Ramayya had 43 sheep. He sold 19. How many remained?

Units: Can we do 3 − 9? NO! 9 is bigger than 3.
So we borrow 1 Ten from the Tens place.
Now Units become: 10 + 3 = 13 🎉
13 − 9 = 4
Tens: 4 became 3 (gave away 1). 3 − 1 = 2
Answer: 43 − 19 = 24 🐑

🔑 Rule: If top Units < bottom Units
→ Borrow 1 Ten → add 10 to Units
→ Tens digit becomes 1 less

Example: 32 − 17

Units: 2 − 7? Can't! Borrow!
10 + 2 = 12.   12 − 7 = 5
Tens: 3 becomes 2.   2 − 1 = 1
Answer: 15 ✅

Example: 44 − 18

Units: 4 − 8? Can't! Borrow!
10 + 4 = 14.   14 − 8 = 6
Tens: 4 becomes 3.   3 − 1 = 2
Answer: 26 ✅

Addition & Subtraction With Zero
Zero is special — it never changes any number!

Adding Zero

A cricket player scored 8 runs in the first over and 0 runs in the next over.

8 + 0 = 8 🏏

If zero is added to any number, the sum is the number itself.

More examples:
10 + 0 = 10
54 + 0 = 54
0 + 17 = 17

Subtracting Zero

Shwetha has ₹9. She does not spend anything.
How many rupees are left? ₹9!

9 − 0 = 9 💰

If zero is subtracted from any number, the difference is the number itself.

More examples:
28 − 0 = 28
53 − 0 = 53

Any number + 0 = that number 🟢
Any number − 0 = that number 🟢

Estimated Sum & Difference
Round to the nearest 10 first, then add or subtract!

How to Round to Nearest 10

🔵 If Units digit is less than 5 → round DOWN
   Example: 32 → 30   (2 < 5, so round down)

🟠 If Units digit is 5 or more → round UP
   Example: 77 → 80   (7 ≥ 5, so round up)

🟡 35 → 40   (5 always rounds up!)

Estimated Sum Example

36 + 51 = ?

36 → 40 (6 ≥ 5, round up)
51 → 50 (1 < 5, round down)
Estimated sum = 40 + 50 = 90 🎯

Estimated Difference Example

26 − 18 = ?

26 → 30 (6 ≥ 5, round up)
18 → 20 (8 ≥ 5, round up)
Estimated difference = 30 − 20 = 10 🎯
