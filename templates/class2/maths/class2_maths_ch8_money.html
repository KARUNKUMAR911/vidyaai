What is Money?
Learn what money is and why we need it!

🛒 Do we need money to buy things?

Yes! We need money to buy things.

Money comes in two forms:
🪙 Coins — made of metal
📄 Currency Notes — made of paper

The value of a coin or note is written on it.

💡 Our currency notes are made of paper.
💡 Coins are made of metal.
💡 The value of a coin or note is written on it.

Making Amounts with Notes & Coins!
Arrange coins and notes to make the right amount!

💡 How do we make ₹6?

We can use ₹1, ₹2 and ₹5 coins only!

Example: ₹2 + ₹2 + ₹2 = ₹6 ✅
Or: ₹5 + ₹1 = ₹6 ✅
Or: ₹2 + ₹2 + ₹1 + ₹1 = ₹6 ✅

🧠 Making ₹20 using ₹10 and ₹5
₹10 + ₹10 = ₹20 ✅
₹10 + ₹5 + ₹5 = ₹20 ✅
₹5 + ₹5 + ₹5 + ₹5 = ₹20 ✅

💡 Making ₹50 using ₹20 and ₹10

Option 1: ₹20 + ₹20 + ₹10 = ₹50 ✅
Option 2: ₹20 + ₹10 + ₹10 + ₹10 = ₹50 ✅

Add & Subtract Money!
Mental addition and subtraction of small amounts!

🍫 The Chocolate Story

A girl asked her Mom for money to buy chocolate.

Mom gave her three ₹5 notes.
She counted: ₹5 + ₹5 + ₹5 = ₹15

At the shop: one chocolate costs ₹4.
She bought 3 chocolates: ₹4 × 3 = ₹12

She paid ₹12 and got ₹3 change back!
₹15 − ₹12 = ₹3 remaining ✅

1. Mom gives money
👩
Take this money and buy the chocolate!
2. Counting money
👧
₹5+₹5+₹5=₹15
3. At the shop
🏪
One chocolate = ₹4
3 chocolates = ₹12
4. Change received
💚
₹15 − ₹12 = ₹3
💡 Money Spent + Change = Money You Had
₹12 + ₹3 = ₹15 ✅

🛒 Transaction using 3-4 Notes

In a transaction (buying something):

💸 You give money to the shopkeeper
💵 The shopkeeper gives you change back

Example: You have ₹30. You buy two items:
🥭 Mango = ₹6
📓 Notebook = ₹12
Total spent = ₹18
Change = ₹30 − ₹18 = ₹12 ✅
