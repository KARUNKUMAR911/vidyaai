Heavy and Light
Some things are heavy. Some things are light!

🌟 Learn This!

We can compare two things to find out which one is heavier and which one is lighter.

👉 A stone is HEAVIER than a feather.

👉 A feather is LIGHTER than a stone.

👉 We write H for HEAVY and L for LIGHT.

Look at the balance scale! ⚖️
🪨Stone
HEAVY
VS
🪶Feather
LIGHT
The heavier side goes DOWN ⬇️
HEAVY = harder to lift 💪
LIGHT = easy to lift 🪶
H = Heavy  |  L = Light

🌟 Activity: Blow and See!

Take a small piece of paper 📄 and an eraser 🧹 — keep them on a table.

💨 Gently blow across the objects.

👉 The paper flew away! The eraser did NOT move.

👉 Paper is lighter than an eraser.

👉 The eraser is heavier than paper.

The lighter thing flies away first when you blow! 💨

💡 The skill of estimation is very important in our daily life! We guess and then we check — just like scientists! 🔬

Lighter things fly away when blown 💨
Heavier things stay on the table 🪨

🌟 Try This at Home!

Before looking, try holding objects in both hands!

👉 Hold a book 📚 in one hand and a pen ✒️ in the other.

👉 Which hand feels heavier? The book does! ✅

👉 The hand that goes DOWN has the heavier thing.

👉 A bottle of water 🧴 is HEAVIER than a ruler 📏.

👉 A chair 🪑 is LIGHTER than a table 🪵.

💡 In the book activity, your teacher says: "Before doing this activity, let children compare weights of different things by holding them in their hands!" That means YOU are a scientist today! 🧪

✋ Hold → Feel → Decide!
Heavier = harder to hold up 💪
Lighter = easy to hold up 🪶
