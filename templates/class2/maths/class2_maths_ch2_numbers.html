Place Value & Face Value
Where a digit sits changes its value!

Place Value vs Face Value

The digit 4 always looks like 4 — that's its Face Value.
But its value depends on WHERE it sits!

T
4
U
4

👉 4 in T place → Place Value = 40 (Forty)
👉 4 in U place → Place Value = 4 (Four)

📝 The number in Units place always has the same face value and place value!

Example: In 54
→ 5 is in Tens place → Place Value = 50, Face Value = 5
→ 4 is in Units place → Place Value = 4, Face Value = 4

Expanded Form
Break a number into tens + units!

Expanded Form

We can expand any number as:
46 = 40 + 6 (4 tens and 6 units)
28 = 20 + 8
99 = 90 + 9

And the short form works backwards:
70 + 9 = 79

Before, After & Between
Find neighbours of numbers!

Before, After & Between

Amar

To get after number: add 1 ➕
To get before number: subtract 1 ➖
The number in the middle = between!

Example: 30, 31, 32
→ 30 is before 31
→ 31 is between 30 and 32
→ 32 is after 31

Bigger & Smaller Numbers
Compare two numbers!

How to Compare

Step 1️⃣: Compare the Tens place first.
Bigger tens digit = Bigger number!

Step 2️⃣: If tens are equal, compare the Units place.

📝 A 2-digit number is always bigger than any 1-digit number!

Example: Compare 48 and 34
→ Tens: 4 vs 3 → 4 is bigger
→ So 48 > 34

Ascending & Descending Order
Arrange numbers from small to big or big to small!

Two Ways to Arrange

⬆️ Ascending Order = Small → Big
(Going UP like climbing stairs!)
Example: 2, 12, 22, 42

⬇️ Descending Order = Big → Small
(Going DOWN like a slide!)
Example: 51, 48, 40, 36

Number Line
Numbers on a line, in order!

What is a Number Line?

A number line starts from 0.
Marks are made at equal distances.
Numbers go in order from left to right.
Numbers to the right are bigger!
Numbers to the left are smaller!
